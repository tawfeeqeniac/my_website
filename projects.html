Sales Analysis Project – Tracking Box-Wise Product Sales

Introduction:

Over a three-month period, I assisted my father in managing his wholesale business, handling responsibilities like stock procurement, billing, logistics, and truck loading/unloading. During this experience, I encountered a real-world data challenge. Although our billing software had a basic sales dashboard, it couldn’t tell us how many full boxes of products were sold. For example, we purchased notebooks from brands like Camlin and Classmate in standard box units (e.g., 1 box = 100 pieces). However, customers would often buy in smaller quantities—such as 20, 30, or 50 pieces. To understand how many boxes' worth of each product were actually sold, I took on the task of analyzing over 100 PDF sales bills. This project involved multiple stages of the data pipeline:

  • Data Acquisition: Extracted raw data from over 100 PDF invoices using Python and pdfminer.
  • Data Cleaning/Transformation: Parsed and structured unorganized text data using regex and pandas.
  • Feature Engineering: Converted individual piece counts into box-equivalents for each product.
  • Data Analysis: Aggregated and analyzed the cleaned data in Excel to generate product-wise and customer-wise sales insights.

This hands-on experience helped me apply core data wrangling techniques to solve a meaningful business problem and derive actionable insights from unstructured data.
